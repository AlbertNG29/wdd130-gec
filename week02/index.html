My City

Brazzaville is the political and administrative capital, as well as the most populous city of the Republic of Congo.

Brazzaville

The city was founded on October 3, 1880, on the site of the former precolonial villages generally referred to by the name Nkuna, the main ones being Mpila and Mfa (or Mfoa). The reasons for the French settlement at this site are multiple: to take advantage of the transshipment point to channel preexisting indigenous trade, to create a French counterpower against the Belgian settlement of Léopoldville on the opposite bank of the Stanley Pool, and to establish a bridgehead along the Congo River to explore and exploit the interior lands.
